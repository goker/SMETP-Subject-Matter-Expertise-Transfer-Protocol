# Legal Domain: Attorney Interview Template

**Duration**: 60-90 minutes
**Role**: Contract Attorney, M&A Specialist, General Counsel, Legal Risk Advisor
**Goal**: Extract contract analysis and legal risk evaluation expertise

---

## Stage 1: Background & Context (5 min)

### Question 1: Legal Background
**Q**: "Tell me about your practice. How many years have you reviewed contracts? What types do you specialize in?"

**Why**: Establishes expertise domain
- Expected: 10+ years, clear practice area (M&A, commercial, employment, IP, etc.)
- Listen for: Mix of deal sizes, industries, complexity levels
- Follow-up: "What's your biggest deal? Most complex contract?"

---

## Stage 2: Contract Review Workflow (15 min)

### Question 2: Initial Review
**Q**: "When you get a new contract, what's the first thing you look at? What's your review process?"

**Why**: Reveals initial screening and priority
- Expected: Contract type, parties, jurisdiction, then specific sections
- Listen for: What triggers red flags immediately
- Example: "First, I identify the parties and jurisdiction. Then liability and indemnification."

### Question 3: Red Flag Sections
**Q**: "For a typical [contract type], what sections do you focus on? Which sections cause the most problems?"

**Why**: Maps critical risk areas
- Expected: Liability, indemnification, termination, IP, payment terms, force majeure
- Listen for: What causes deals to break or litigation
- Follow-up: "What's hidden in those sections that people miss?"

### Question 4: Standard vs Custom
**Q**: "What does a market-standard contract look like for [your specialty]? What deviations trigger concern?"

**Why**: Identifies baseline and risk tolerance
- Expected: Knowledge of market norms, what's favorable vs unfavorable
- Listen for: How they balance aggressive positioning vs market practice
- Example: "Standard limitation of liability is 1x annual fees. 5x is aggressive. Unlimited is a deal-breaker."

---

## Stage 3: Legal Risk Assessment (20 min)

### Question 5: Risk Scoring
**Q**: "How do you assess risk in a contract? Do you have a mental ranking from safe to dangerous?"

**Why**: Reveals risk stratification
- Expected: Scale of risk (safe, manageable, concerning, dangerous, unacceptable)
- Listen for: Thresholds that trigger escalation
- Example: "Unlimited liability = unacceptable. Capped at 1x = safe. Uncapped indemnity = concerning."

### Question 6: Liability & Indemnification
**Q**: "Walk me through your approach to liability and indemnification clauses. What's the worst case in each?"

**Why**: Extracts logic for highest-impact clauses
- Expected: Who bears risk, what triggers liability, caps, exclusions
- Listen for: How they negotiate these
- Example: "I always cap indemnification at insurance limits. No caps = negotiation required."

### Question 7: Negotiation Playbook
**Q**: "Give me a clause that typically needs negotiation. What's your opening position vs walk-away position?"

**Why**: Captures negotiation heuristics
- Expected: Specific clause, their positioning, walk-away threshold
- Listen for: Which issues are hills to die on vs negotiable
- Example: "IP indemnity: I want broad protection. I'll accept market language but not 'any and all claims'."

### Question 8: Dangerous Combinations
**Q**: "Are there combinations of clauses that are particularly dangerous? Issues that interact?"

**Why**: Reveals complex reasoning
- Expected: Interactions between liability, indemnity, termination, payment
- Listen for: Second-order effects
- Example: "If you have unlimited indemnity AND no termination rights AND automatic renewal = you're trapped."

---

## Stage 4: Legal Heuristics & Patterns (20 min)

### Question 9: Market Deviations
**Q**: "What 'market-standard deviations' do you notice that signal inexperience or aggressive positioning?"

**Why**: Captures pattern recognition
- Expected: Language choices that signal drafting skill, risk appetite
- Listen for: Red flags in wording itself ("any and all", "including but not limited to")
- Example: "If they use 'any and all claims' instead of 'claims arising from', they're aggressive. I prepare accordingly."

### Question 10: Hidden Risks
**Q**: "What risks do business people miss in contracts they think are 'standard'?"

**Why**: Captures tacit expertise
- Expected: Common traps, counter-intuitive risks
- Listen for: What kills deals quietly vs obviously
- Example: "Most miss the interaction between termination for cause and severance obligations."

### Question 11: Industry-Specific Patterns
**Q**: "Are there industry-specific risks or patterns? What's different between tech contracts vs manufacturing?"

**Why**: Reveals domain-specific knowledge
- Expected: Different risk profiles, standards, issues
- Listen for: What changes by industry
- Example: "In SaaS, IP indemnity is bigger deal. In manufacturing, supply chain resilience is bigger."

### Question 12: Deal-Breaker Patterns
**Q**: "Tell me about a deal you killed or pushed back hard on. What was the pattern?"

**Why**: Reveals judgment boundaries
- Expected: Specific clause or combination that triggered "no"
- Listen for: Where they draw the line
- Example: "Unlimited indemnity + no insurance requirements + no termination rights = I told client to walk"

---

## Stage 5: Historical Validation (15 min)

### Question 13: Case Studies
**Q**: "Give me 5 contracts you've reviewed — 2 you approved easily, 2 you negotiated, 1 you rejected or pushed back hard. The issues you identified."

**Why**: Provides test cases
- Capture: Contract type, parties, jurisdiction, key issues, your assessment, outcome
- Example format:
  ```
  Contract: SaaS Services Agreement
  Parties: TechCorp → Enterprise Customer
  Your assessment: Moderate risk (indemnity aggressive, liability caps OK)
  Key issues: IP indemnity overly broad, automatic renewal 3 years
  Your recommendation: Renegotiate indemnity, shorten renewal
  Outcome: Customer negotiated. Worked fine.
  ```

### Question 14: Accuracy & Hindsight
**Q**: "Of contracts you reviewed, do any come back to haunt you later? Clauses you missed?"

**Why**: Calibrates accuracy and humility
- Expected: Few surprises if expertise is real
- Listen for: What catches them off-guard
- Follow-up: "What did you miss and why?"

---

## Stage 6: Edge Cases & Special Situations (10 min)

### Question 15: Unusual Parties
**Q**: "How does your approach change for: startups vs enterprises, foreign parties, nonprofit, government, joint ventures?"

**Why**: Reveals party-specific logic
- Expected: Different risk tolerance, different negotiation power
- Listen for: How analysis changes based on counterparty
- Example: "For startups, I'm more concerned about solvency. For enterprises, I worry about bureaucracy."

### Question 16: Jurisdiction & Governing Law
**Q**: "How important is governing law and jurisdiction? Which are advantageous? Which should trigger concern?"

**Why**: Reveals cross-border expertise
- Expected: Knowledge of jurisdiction-specific risks
- Listen for: Which jurisdictions they prefer, which they avoid
- Example: "Delaware is neutral. California is plaintiff-friendly. International = complexity."

### Question 17: Confidence Calibration
**Q**: "When are you 95% confident a contract is safe? When are you 60% confident?"

**Why**: Captures confidence levels
- Expected: Different confidence for market-standard vs bespoke vs unusual
- Listen for: When they escalate to specialist counsel
- Example: "95% on market standard. 70% on bespoke. 40% on cross-border IP = get specialist."

---

## Stage 7: Closing (5 min)

### Question 18: Teaching New Associates
**Q**: "If you were training a junior associate on contract review for your specialty, what would be the top 5 things to check?"

**Why**: Forces prioritization
- Expected: Most critical risks in priority order
- Listen for: What they teach first vs last

### Question 19: Blind Spots
**Q**: "What challenges do you face? What would break your analysis?"

**Why**: Reveals limitations
- Expected: Rapid change in law, new business models, complex interactions
- Listen for: What keeps them up at night

## Follow-Up Questions

- "What single clause is most important in [your specialty]?"
- "How do you handle aggressive counterparties?"
- "What percentage of deals get escalated to you vs handled by juniors?"
- "How do you balance legal perfection vs business reality?"
- "What's the weirdest/most dangerous contract you've seen?"

---

## Notes for Interviewer

1. **Market knowledge** — Listen for specific understanding of market standards
2. **Risk quantification** — Can they articulate financial risk? "Unlimited indemnity on $10M contract = unacceptable"
3. **Negotiation playbook** — What's their positioning strategy?
4. **Pattern recognition** — What phrases or structures signal problems?
5. **Party-specific logic** — How do they adjust for counterparty type?
6. **Outcomes validation** — Have their contracts actually worked out or caused problems?
